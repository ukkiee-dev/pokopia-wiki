Important Requests

Throughout the game, you will work with Professor Tangrowth and the Pokï¿½mon in each area to fulfill Important Requests. These are the story of the game and have you help out the various Pokï¿½mon in each area to help bring the environment level up and your Trainer Rank. With each completion, your Trainer Rank will increased further.

Each area also has an Important Request to rebuild the Pokï¿½mon Center.

Yawn Up A Storm

As you work through restoring the Withered Wastelands, it's noted that it hasn't rained in the area in so long and so it's suggested to find a Pokï¿½mon that can make it rain. This Pokï¿½mon is Slowpoke and you need to set up the habitat for Slowpoke to appear.

When done, Slowpoke will offer its assistance but note that in order to make it rain, the humidity of the area has to reach a point that its yawn will cause the rain. To do so, you will need to do the following: You will need to use Water Gun to liven up the water as much as possible in the area. You can break rocks to create rivers that help increase the mmoisture. Create various flower patches to help increase the humidity too.

Once the humidity reaches 100, get Slowpoke to yawn. It will cause a small amount of rain but it won't last. However, a noise is heard from the coast. Go there and you will find Kyogre, who got alerted by your attempt to make it rain and has arrived and will make it rain entirely, which moistens up the entire surrounding area as the rain hits.

Do the...Team Initiation Challenge

As you progress through the story, you learn of the Team Intiiation Challenge,a strange building on the coast of the Withered Wastelands. A machine on the building will give you a variety of tasks for you to complete and is part of the "Do the Team Initation Challenge request

There are nine different challenges to go through which require bringing a variety of items to the building, typically items found as you progres through the game. Each time you complete it, you will get a badge.

Brighten Things Up

In Bleak Beach, there is perpetual darkness regardless of if it is day or night. After helping Meowth with a few tasks to help brighten the area using Generate with Pawmi. Soon, Trubbish will hear a noise behind a wall. Break it down and you'll find Mosslax

Trubbish will recommend that Professor Tangrowth's help on how to wake it up. Bring Tangrowth and it will postulate that the reason Snorlax hasn't woken up is because it is too dark and recommends you start brightening the area up. Start lighting the area up to bring it up and you can check with Tangrowth to see how bright it is.

Once you've helped Torchic with another task, you will find the Charging Station. Get the brightness up enough and two flowers will bloom on Snorlax's head and Tangrowth feels the task here is working and to get more flowers to bloom will wake it up. Soon after, a wounded Pokï¿½mon is found nearby, a pale Pikachu called Peakychu. Peakychu is confused as to where it is and wonders where its friends are but comes over to help and suggests makig seashell lamps in the cave to lighten it up even more.

Peakychu will say that if there's enough power, it will be able to illuminate the area and brings you to a windmill to help connect the town up to fully brighten it up. Connect power stations up and activate furnaces and water wheels to generate enough power. When enough power is generated, Peakychu will use Illuminate to finally brighten up the area. This attracts the attention of Raikou which unleashes a thunder which wakes Mosslax up.

Time To Party

In Rocky Ridges, after you have rescued Chef Dente from the barrel it was stuck in, and have found DJ Rotom in a cave, Rotom will be excited about building a town with fun vibes and the two figure out the way to bring Pokï¿½mon would be to have a big party, and the request begins. As you get a Party Pot, you need to bring the mood of the area up to 100.

Your first task given by DJ Rotom is to find a CD, which can be found around the area. Bring the CD to DJ Rotom and it will play it. Then, Professor Tangrowth will ask for you to find a Lost Relic (Large) for him to appraise. When appraised, it will ask for you to place it as a decoration for the party.

Next, Rotom suggests finding a Pokï¿½mon with the Hype ability such as Ludicolo. Go make the habitat for Ludicolo and bring Ludicolo to the party. With that, Chef Dente then suggests making a Party Platter, so offers the recipe to make it, and asks for you to make 5 with varied meals within. Bring the Party Platters to the Party pot and place them with the food.

The final task is to bring the Mood up to 100. Keep checking with Rotom to find the mood, and keep adding more Lost Relics around, as well as items such as Balloons and even finding more Pokï¿½mon with Hype to help bring the points up.

When the mood is there, it's time to cook the meal, which requires you to bring 15 Leppa Berry, 15 Wheat, 15 Beans and 5 Honey, then bring 5 Pokï¿½mon including Chef Dente. The party will begin and Volcanion will appear, surprised. It will be happy at the festivities and set off fireworks.

Rebuild the Huge Building

In Sparkling Skylands, you will find a lot of scrap areas and eventually find Tinkamaster, a Tinkaton that is veru curious about machines from humans and thinks it can fix up a massive broken building in the Skylands. It asks for your help in creating things for it. It starts off needing concrete which you can get from Conkeldurr with the mixing machine. When done, Tinkaton will want your help to get the lifts working in the area and gives Tinkagears to help.

Soon, you will get to the building where Tinkaton thinks it will be able to build, but to do so as with all buildings you will need to get resources. To build 2F you need 25 Concrete, 10 Glass, 5 Pokï¿½metal and 10 Iron Ingots, as well as 6 Pokï¿½mon which include Professor Tangrowth.

Build that and your next task is to build the third floor which needs 25 Concrete, 10 Glass, 10 GLowing Stone and 15 Copper Ingots, as well as 10 Pokï¿½mon with a recommendation that you also bring Chef Dente to help out set up a restaurant for Pokï¿½mon and people to enjoy.

Finally, the fourth floor requires 40 Concrete, 10 Glass, 10 Paper, 10 Brick and 10 Lumber, as well as 15 Pokï¿½mon including the recommendation of Peakychu to come as it's considered an outgoing Pokï¿½mon. Build it and the floor is complete

After completion, a noise is heard on the roof as a portal seems to appear. Go to the roof and you find a Master Ball which soon opens to unleash Mewtwo, who is surprised at no humans being around as it was concerned they were back, but is impressed that you managed to build the skycraper
